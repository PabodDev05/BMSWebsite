ABOUT US

From Humble Beginnings to Academic Brilliance - The journey of Model Boy's College

Our History

Boy's Model College, formerly known as Malabe Boys' School, was founded in 1987 by W.K. Dep. Initially established as a primary school with a handful of students and teachers, the institution has since grown exponentially. Over the years, the school expanded its facilities and curriculum to cater to secondary and higher education, becoming one of the most prominent educational institutions in the region.

In 1995, the school introduced Advanced Level classes, and by 2000, it had established a reputation for excellence in both academic and extracurricular activities. The school's commitment to holistic education has led to numerous accolades in sports, arts, and community service.

Today, Boy's Model College boasts over 200 teaching staff and more than 5000 students, providing a well-rounded education that emphasizes academic excellence, character development, and community involvement.

Our Values

At Boy's Model College, we are committed to fostering a culture of integrity, respect, and excellence. Our motto "අත්තාහි අත්තනෝ නාථො" reflects our belief in self-reliance and personal responsibility.

Milestones

1987

Inaugurated by Hon. Mr. Ranil Wickramasinghe on May 28, 1987. Mr. W.K. Dep was the founder and the first principal.

1991

Completed the Lalith Ethulathmudali Hall, a new three-story building, on May 1, 1991.

1996

School had classes up to grade ten.

2003

Awarded the Suvasara Thahshalava Award for being the cleanest school on October 16, 2003.

2006

Opened a computer laboratory and launched the official school website on August 2, 2006.

2007

Promoted to a Maha Vidyalaya (high school) on May 4, 2007, and opened a science lab on September 21, 2007.

Our Houses

Students at Boy's Model College are divided into four houses, each fostering a sense of community and healthy competition.
